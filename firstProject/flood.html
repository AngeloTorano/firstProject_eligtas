Floods are when water overflows from the normal boundaries of a stream, river or other body of water, or accumulates in an area that is usually dry. There are two main types of floods. Inundation floods are slow and develop over hours or days. Flash floods occur suddenly, often without warning and usually due to heavy rain. Though annual flooding is a natural phenomenon in many parts of the world, human habitation and land-use practices have led to an increase in frequency and size of floods.

Prepare NOW

  • Be up to date with the latest flood or rainfall news.

  • Buy flashlights with extra batteries to use in emergency situations.

  • Keep your valuables in waterproof containers.

  • If time allows, move furniture and other important items to the upper floors of your home.

  • Follow the route instructed by local authorities for evacuation.

Survive DURING

  • If you are at home, gather in the designated safe area with your emergency go bag and other essential supplies.

  • If you live in such an area, evacuate to higher ground to avoid the risk of flash floods.

  • Tune in to a reliable AM radio station or use battery-operated radios to stay informed about the latest weather updates and road conditions.

  • Cancel all travel and outdoor activities during a typhoon.

  • Avoid wading through floodwaters to prevent waterborne diseases and electric shock from submerged power lines.

  • It's normal to feel anxious and distressed during a typhoon, but try to remain calm and composed.

Be Safe AFTER

  • Check for any structural damage to your house and potential hazards like fallen trees or power lines.

  • Inspect the emergency supplies you prepared before the typhoon to ensure they are intact and usable.

  • Keep your radio tuned to an AM station for official updates on the weather conditions and post-typhoon advisories.

  • Boil tap water before consuming it or rely on bottled water until authorities declare tap water safe to drink.

  • Stay updated on weather forecasts to prepare for any potential aftershocks or subsequent storms that may affect your area.

  • Don't hesitate to seek help or offer assistance to those in need.
